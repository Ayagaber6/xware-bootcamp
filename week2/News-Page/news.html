On This Day in History

Good News in History,July28

83 years ago today, small iron and bronze fragments found during the Sutton Hoo ship burial were determined to be part of a kingly helmet. Buried around 625 and is widely associated with King Rædwald of East Anglia, its elaborate decoration may have given it a secondary function akin to a crown. The helmet was also a functional piece of armor that would have offered considerable protection if ever used in warfare. The Sutton Hoo Helmet has been called one of the most spectacular archeological discoveries ever made, and a symbol of the discipline writ large.
